# Security Glossary

This glossary defines common cybersecurity terms
used throughout networking, web security,
pentesting, blue team, and cloud environments.

The definitions are short, practical, and consistent.

---

## Asset
Anything that has value and must be protected.
Examples: data, systems, credentials.

---

## Authentication
The process of verifying identity.
Answers: *Who are you?*

---

## Authorization
The process of verifying permissions.
Answers: *What are you allowed to do?*

---

## Attack Surface
All points where an attacker can interact with a system.

---

## Availability
Ensuring systems and data are accessible when needed.

---

## Blue Team
The defensive side responsible for detection,
monitoring, and incident response.

---

## CIA Triad
Core security principles:
- Confidentiality
- Integrity
- Availability

---

## Client
A system or application that sends requests to a server.

---

## Confidentiality
Protecting data from unauthorized access.

---

## Domain
A logical boundary in Active Directory
used to manage users and resources.

---

## Endpoint
A device connected to a network,
such as a workstation or server.

---

## Exploit
A method used to take advantage of a vulnerability.

---

## Firewall
A system that controls network traffic
based on defined rules.

---

## Incident
An event that threatens confidentiality,
integrity, or availability.

---

## Integrity
Ensuring data is accurate and not improperly modified.

---

## Least Privilege
Granting only the minimum access required
to perform a task.

---

## Logging
The process of recording system and user activity.

---

## Malware
Malicious software designed to harm systems,
steal data, or gain unauthorized access.

---

## Network
A group of connected systems
that communicate with each other.

---

## Pentesting
Authorized security testing
to identify weaknesses.

---

## Red Team
A team that simulates real attackers
to test defenses and detection.

---

## Risk
The combination of asset value,
threat likelihood, and vulnerability impact.

---

## Server
A system that listens for requests
and provides services.

---

## SIEM
Security Information and Event Management system
used for log collection and correlation.

---

## Threat
Anything that can cause harm
to a system or organization.

---

## Vulnerability
A weakness that can be exploited
by a threat.

---

## Key Takeaways

- shared terminology improves communication
- short definitions improve clarity
- glossary supports consistency across notes
- understanding terms is foundational
